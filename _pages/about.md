Haisa Osmanli is a Principal Data Scientist and Statistician specializing in advanced analytics, causal inference, and predictive modeling. With a PhD in Statistics and extensive experience across government and research environments, his work focuses on transforming complex data into actionable insights that support high-level decision-making and policy development.

Her expertise spans large-scale administrative data analysis, survival modeling, and AI-driven solutions, particularly in domains such as mental health, addictions, and public systems. He has developed and applied rigorous statistical methodologies—including propensity score matching, logistic regression, and time series analysis—to uncover meaningful patterns and inform evidence-based interventions.

Haisa’s work is defined by a strong commitment to bridging theory and practice, combining methodological rigor with real-world impact. Through her research and collaborations, she contributes to advancing data-driven solutions that address complex societal challenges while fostering innovation across interdisciplinary settings.
