I am a Principal Data Scientist and Statistician working at the intersection of advanced analytics, public policy, and real-world impact. With a PhD in Statistics and extensive experience across government, research, and consulting environments, I specialize in transforming complex, high-dimensional data into actionable insights that inform decision-making at the highest levels. My work spans predictive modeling, causal inference, survival analysis, and AI-driven solutions, with a strong emphasis on translating technical results into clear, strategic narratives for diverse audiences.

My research and applied work focus on socially meaningful domains, including mental health, addictions, economic resilience, and public service systems. I have led and contributed to projects involving large-scale administrative data, designed novel statistical models, and implemented methodologies such as propensity score matching, logistic regression, and time series analysis to uncover patterns that drive policy innovation. I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between rigorous statistical theory and practical implementation, ensuring that analytical solutions are both methodologically sound and operationally impactful.

Beyond technical expertise, I bring a strong commitment to thought leadership, interdisciplinary collaboration, and continuous learning. I actively engage in academic and applied research initiatives, including projects on AI applications in economic systems and resilience modeling. Through this platform, I share my work, ideas, and contributions with the broader community, aiming to foster collaboration, inspire innovation, and contribute to evidence-based solutions that address complex societal challenges.
