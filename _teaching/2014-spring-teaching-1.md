Designed and delivered an intensive graduate-level course on supervised and unsupervised machine learning with applications in epidemiology.
Developed a theory-driven curriculum covering empirical risk minimization, optimization (gradient descent), and generalization theory.
Taught core models including linear and logistic regression, generalized linear models (Poisson), and regularization techniques (Lasso, Ridge, Elastic Net).
Delivered instruction on unsupervised learning methods, including clustering, PCA, and anomaly detection.
Integrated real-world epidemiological applications such as incidence modeling and outbreak detection.
Mentored students on the interplay between machine learning, statistical inference, and mechanistic models (e.g., SIR/SIS).
